Along the Trent in Campbellford

~3 hours · Walk and drive · Easy to Park

Campbellford is shaped by water. The Trent River runs straight through town, powering stations, feeding locks, and slowing down just enough in parks and bends to invite you to stop. A few hours here means walking bridges, watching the lock gates open and close, cutting through Old Mill Park, and noticing small, almost accidental details along the way. It’s not a checklist town — it’s a follow-the-river kind of place.

The Trent River flows steadily past the Ranney Falls Generating Station, where industry and water meet without fuss.

Trees line the river near Ranney Falls, softening the current and quieting the scene.

Rocky riverbanks rise sharply, trees clinging to the rugged walls above the flowing water.

The steel bridge lifts open, pausing traffic as boats prepare to pass.

Beyond the bridge, the canal stretches forward, clean lines bordered by grass.

Closer now, the lock comes into focus—functional, precise, quietly impressive.

Good to Know

Closing

Once you’ve crossed the bridges, traced the canal, and looped back through town, Campbellford feels complete without being finished. The river keeps moving, the lock keeps working, and the town carries on at its own scale. It’s an easy stop, but one that rewards staying a little longer than planned.

Leaving town to the south, cornfields stretch out, and Campbellford slowly fades into memory.
